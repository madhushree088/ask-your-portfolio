# Investment Glossary

**P&L (Profit and Loss)**: The difference between the current value of an investment and its original cost. Positive P&L means profit, negative means loss.

**Sector Allocation**: The distribution of a portfolio's value across different industry sectors, expressed as a percentage of total portfolio value.

**EBITDA**: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. A measure of a company's core profitability.

**NAV (Net Asset Value)**: The per-unit value of a mutual fund or ETF, calculated as total assets minus liabilities divided by total units.

**Beta**: A measure of a stock's volatility relative to the overall market. Beta > 1 means more volatile than the market.

**Dividend Yield**: Annual dividend per share divided by the stock's current price, expressed as a percentage.

**PE Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)**: The ratio of a company's stock price to its earnings per share. Used to assess valuation.

**Market Capitalization**: The total market value of a company's outstanding shares (price × total shares).

**Portfolio Diversification**: Spreading investments across different assets, sectors, or geographies to reduce risk.

**Average Cost / Cost Basis**: The average price paid per share across all purchases of a stock.

**Unrealized Gain/Loss**: Profit or loss on an investment that has not yet been sold.

**Realized Gain/Loss**: Profit or loss that has been locked in by selling an investment.

**HNI (High Net-worth Individual)**: An individual with investable assets typically exceeding ₹5 crore in India.

**Large Cap**: Companies with market capitalization above ₹20,000 crore. Generally considered stable investments.

**Mid Cap**: Companies with market capitalization between ₹5,000–20,000 crore.

**Small Cap**: Companies with market capitalization below ₹5,000 crore. Higher risk, higher potential return.
