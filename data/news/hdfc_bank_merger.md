# HDFC Bank Post-Merger Integration Update

HDFC Bank completed its merger with HDFC Ltd in July 2023, creating one of the world's largest banks by market cap. The integration is progressing well, with the bank focusing on cross-selling home loans through its branch network.

Loan growth moderated to 15% YoY as the bank prioritized deposit mobilization to improve its CD (credit-deposit) ratio. NIM (Net Interest Margin) dipped slightly to 3.4% due to higher cost of funds post-merger.

The stock has underperformed peers in FY2024, down roughly 8% YTD, leading some investors to trim positions. However, long-term fundamentals remain intact with a strong retail franchise and asset quality.

Management guided for NIM stabilization and gradual CD ratio improvement over the next 4–6 quarters.
